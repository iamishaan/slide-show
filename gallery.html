The BLUE BIRD

The year of 2013 saw rapid modernization in Falcons’ history when the entire aircraft was manufactured from scratch by the team members themselves. Incorporating new cutting edge technologies, Structure testing mechanisms and computer aided testing, Blue Bird was an amalgamation of modernization and historic data achieved by Falcons. It was responsible to take Falcons’ into the elite class of SAE teams by achieving an overall rank of 11th and finishing first in Asia. It was the dawn of a new era in VIT’s aero student chapters.

The Carvao

Carvao is a Medium Range Long Endurance (MALE) aircraft under the classification provided by DGCA. Fitted with onboard telemetry systems and First person view camera, the aircraft can be used remotely in a wide range of mission scenarios. The aircraft can be flown hands-free by predefining the mission statement. With a versatile payload bay, the aircraft is capable of transporting both static and dynamic payloads with a maximum payload of 28 lbs. Featuring a wide wing span for optimal endurance and glide performance, the aircraft is highly efficient to boost the range. The robust nature of the aircraft can be attributed to the carbon fiber composites constituting the frame of the aircraft. Carvao can be carry either a glow engine or a electric motor as a source of its power-plant.

The FALCONS ALPHA

The first of its class from the Falcons’ workstation was Falcons Alpha. It was a beautiful amalgamation of aerodynamics and structures built to meet its optimum output under the given conditions by the Society of Automotive Engineers (SAE).Falcons Alpha was a cargo plane to lift nearly 1.5 times its original weight controlled by a 2.4 GHz transmitter from a ground source Designed to compete at FortWorth, Texas in 2011 Falcons Alpha gave Falcons’ a historic flight making it the first ever aircraft built by VIT’s own aero team to touch the American sky and finished at 37th of the 75 teams.
